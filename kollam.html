KOLLAM


Kollam is the fourth largest city in Kerala and the fifth largest in terms of corporation area. It is known for cashew processing and coir manufacturing. Ashtamudi Lake is considered the southern gateway to the backwaters of Kerala and is a prominent tourist destination at Kollam.

Theevalli palace

A place of great historic importance, Thevally Palace is situated on the banks of Ashtamude Lake. Once served as the home of Maharaja of Travancore, it was constructed under the rule of Gauri Parvathi Bai between 1800 and 1819. The striking feature about the palace is that it presents an intriguing fusion of Dutch, English and Portuguese style of architecture.

Kollam Beach

Kollam beach or Mahatma Gandhi Beach is considered to be the best destination for weddings in Kerala. This beach if one of the few beaches of India which has a lifeguard outpost. The Kollam Port is renowned for the famous international cashew trade and is the second largest after the Port of Cochin.
